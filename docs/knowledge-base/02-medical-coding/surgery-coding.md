# Surgery Coding

## Overview

Surgical coding involves the assignment of CPT codes from the Surgery section (10021-69990) to describe surgical procedures performed by physicians. Surgical coding requires understanding of the global surgical package, global periods, multiple procedure reductions, and the specific anatomy and techniques involved.

---

## The Global Surgical Package

The global surgical package (also called "global period" or "global fee") includes all necessary services before, during, and after a surgical procedure. Medicare defines three types of global packages.

### Components Included in the Global Package

1. **Pre-operative services**:
   - Pre-operative visit (day before or day of surgery for major procedures)
   - History and physical focused on the surgical condition
   - Obtaining informed consent
   - Pre-operative evaluation and management

2. **Intra-operative services**:
   - The surgical procedure itself
   - Local infiltration, digital block, or topical anesthesia
   - Opening and closing of the surgical site
   - Application of splints, casts, or steri-strips
   - Surgical supplies and equipment ordinarily used

3. **Post-operative services**:
   - Follow-up visits during the global period
   - Routine post-operative care
   - Wound checks, suture/staple removal
   - Post-operative pain management
   - Inpatient post-surgical management
   - Treatment of minor complications (not requiring return to OR)

### Services NOT Included in the Global Package (Separately Billable)

- New or worsened condition requiring a return to the operating room
- E/M services unrelated to the surgery (use modifier 24)
- E/M that results in the decision to perform surgery (use modifier 57)
- Treatment of complications requiring return to OR (use modifier 78)
- Staged or planned procedures (use modifier 58)
- Diagnostic procedures not part of the surgery
- Care for an underlying condition that is not the surgical condition

## Global Periods

### 000-Day Global (Minor Procedures)

- **Duration**: 0 days (same day only)
- **Examples**: Endoscopy, skin biopsy, joint injection, suture removal, catheter insertion
- **Billing**: E/M can be billed on same day with modifier 25 if significant and separately identifiable
- **Post-op visits**: Not included; each visit is separately billable (if medically necessary)

### 010-Day Global (Simple Procedures)

- **Duration**: 10 days post-procedure
- **Examples**: Skin lesion excision, hernia repair (some), cataract surgery
- **Billing**: Post-op visits within 10 days are included unless for an unrelated condition
- **Return to OR**: If the patient returns to the OR for a related condition within 10 days, use modifier 78

- **Duration**: 90 days post-procedure
- **Examples**: Total joint replacement, open heart surgery, spinal fusion, colectomy, hysterectomy
- **Billing**: All post-op care within 90 days is included

- **Pre-op E/M**: Use modifier 57 if E/M results in decision for surgery

## Multiple Surgery Reduction

### Medicare Rule

When multiple surgical procedures are performed by the same physician during the same session, Medicare applies:

| Order | Payment |
|-------|---------|
| First (highest priced) | 100% of fee schedule |
| Second (next highest) | 50% |
| Third and subsequent | 25% |

**Important**: The order is determined by the fee schedule amount, NOT the CPT code order or clinical importance. The system automatically sorts by fee schedule amount.

### Exceptions

- **Add-on codes**: Always paid at 100%, never reduced
- **Bilateral modifier 50**: Paid at 150% (already adjusted)
- **Assistant surgeon**: Paid at 20-25% of reduced allowance
- **Co-surgeon**: Paid at 62.5% each (total 125%)

### Example Calculation

**Scenario**: Same-operative-session procedures:
1. 27130 (THA) -- fee $1,400
2. 27372 (Hardware removal) -- fee $500
3. 29824 (Distal clavicle excision) -- fee $600

**Payment**:
- 27130: $1,400 (100%) = $1,400
- 29824: $600 (50%) = $300
- 27372: $500 (25%) = $125
- **Total**: $1,825

### Private Payer Variations

Some commercial payers:
- Use their own reduction percentages (e.g., 100/100/75)
- Require modifier 51 on secondary procedures
- Do not apply multiple reduction to E/M or diagnostic services
- May use a "multiple surgery indicator" file specific to their system

## Assistant Surgeon Billing

| Modifier | Type | Payment |
|----------|------|---------|
| 80 | Physician assistant surgeon | 20% of fee schedule |
| 81 | Minimum assistant surgeon | Varies (typically 10-15%) |
| 82 | Assistant surgeon (resident unavailable) | 20% (teaching hospitals) |
| AS | Non-physician (PA, NP, CNS) | 85% of PA fee schedule |

**Rules**:
- Assistant surgeon is NOT allowed for all procedures (check MUE/NCCI)
- Teaching hospitals: Assistant surgeon (modifier 82) requires documentation of resident unavailability
- The assistant uses the same CPT code as the primary surgeon + modifier

## Co-Surgery (Modifier 62)

**Definition**: Two surgeons (typically from different specialties) perform distinct parts of a single procedure.

**Example**: An orthopedic surgeon performs the bone work and a plastic surgeon performs the soft tissue coverage in a complex limb salvage procedure. Both bill 27880 (amputation, leg) with modifier 62.

**Payment**: 62.5% of fee schedule to EACH surgeon (125% total).
**Documentation**: The operative note must clearly delineate each surgeon's role.

---

## Surgical Team (Modifier 66)

**Definition**: A highly complex procedure requiring multiple surgeons of different specialties and supporting personnel.

**Example**: Multi-organ transplant, conjoined twin separation.

**Payment**: Determined by the Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC) on a case-by-case basis.

## Surgery Coding Denial Reasons and Solutions

| Denial Reason | Common Cause | Solution |
|---------------|--------------|----------|
| Global period not observed | E/M billed during global without modifier 24, 57, 78, or 79 | Check global period before billing, append correct modifier |
| Bundling (NCCI edit) | Component code billed with comprehensive code | Use modifier 59 or -XS if distinct |
| Medical necessity | Diagnosis does not support the procedure | Verify ICD-10 code supports the surgery |
| Prior authorization not obtained | Required but not obtained | Check payer policy before scheduling |
| Assistant surgeon not allowed | Code listed as "assistant not allowed" | Check Medically Unlikely Edit |
| Modifier 50 vs. separate lines | Incorrect bilateral billing | Check bilateral indicator in fee schedule |
| Multiple procedure reduction not applied | Billing without modifier 51 | Check payer requirements |
| Incorrect conversion | Lap-to-open conversion billed as two codes | Bill only the open code |
| Unlisted code without documentation | No operative note submitted | Submit report with unlisted claim |

## Q&A Pairs

**Q:** A surgeon performs a laparoscopic cholecystectomy (47562) but converts to open cholecystectomy (47600) due to dense adhesions. What code(s) should be billed?

**A:** Bill only 47600 (open cholecystectomy) with modifier 22 (Increased Procedural Service) since the conversion from laparoscopic to open represents additional work. Do NOT bill both 47562 and 47600. The laparoscopic portion is included in the open procedure. Document the reason for conversion and the additional time required.

---

**Q:** What is the global period for a total hip arthroplasty (27130) and what services are included?

**A:** 27130 has a 090-day global period. Included services are:
- One pre-operative visit (typically the day before or day of surgery)
- The surgical procedure itself
- All post-operative medical and surgical management for 90 days
- Wound checks, suture removal, physical therapy visits
- Routine follow-up X-rays during the 90 days
- Treatment of minor complications (not requiring return to OR)

Excluded (separately billable): Treatment of major complications, E/M for unrelated conditions (modifier 24), planned staged procedures (modifier 58), diagnostic procedures not part of the surgery.

---

**Q:** A patient has a right knee arthroscopy with medial meniscectomy (29881-RT) and a left knee arthroscopy with lateral meniscectomy (29881-LT) during the same session. How should this be billed?

**A:** Medicare allows two lines:
- 29881-RT (100% of fee schedule)
- 29881-LT (50% for second procedure)

Some payers prefer:
- 29881-50 (bilateral) -- pay 150% of one fee schedule

Check the specific payer policy. For Medicare, use two lines with RT and LT since 29881 has a bilateral indicator of 1 (bilateral surgery adjustment applies). Report the higher-valued side on one line and the other as the secondary.

---

**Q:** A surgeon performs a colectomy (44140) and the patient develops a wound infection requiring return to the OR for I&D on post-op day 7. How should the return to OR be coded?

**A:** The return to the OR for I&D of a wound infection related to the original surgery should be billed with the appropriate I&D code (e.g., 10180 for complex wound I&D) with modifier 78 (Return to the OR for a related procedure during the postoperative period). The documentation must state the I&D was for a complication of the original surgery and was unplanned. Payment is at approximately 80% of the fee schedule.

---

**Q:** What is the difference between excision (11400-11646) and destruction (17000-17286)?

**A:** 
- **Excision**: Full-thickness removal of a lesion with margins, typically followed by closure. The lesion is sent for pathology.
- **Destruction**: Tissue is destroyed (cautery, cryotherapy, laser, electrosurgery) in situ. No specimen for pathology.

Excision codes require:
- Measurement of lesion + excision margins (2+ dimensions)
- Location
- Whether benign (11400) or malignant (11600)
- Pathologic confirmation of the diagnosis

Destruction codes are based on lesion count and location only (e.g., 17000 = first premalignant lesion, 17003 = 2-14 lesions).

---

**Q:** How are multiple skin lesion excisions on the same date coded?

**A:** Each lesion is coded separately:
1. List the highest-valued excision first
2. Append modifier 59 or -XS to subsequent excisions if in separate anatomic sites
3. Use modifiers LT, RT, or specific body area modifiers (FA-F9) for different lesions

**Example**: Excision of malignant lesion on left cheek (11643-LT) and benign lesion on right arm (11402-RT). Bill both. The second excision (regardless of billing order) is subject to multiple procedure reduction (50%).

---

**Q:** A patient has a total knee replacement (27447) on the right knee. Four weeks later, the same patient falls and fractures the left ankle, requiring ORIF (27829). How is the ankle surgery coded during the knee replacement's global period?

**A:** Bill 27829-79 (Unrelated procedure during postoperative period). The ankle surgery is completely unrelated to the knee replacement. Modifier 79 indicates the second surgery is not related to the original procedure and is not included in the global package. The ankle procedure is paid at 100% (no reduction since it is a different procedure).

---

**Q:** When is a diagnostic surgical code (e.g., diagnostic colonoscopy 45378) reduced or bundled with a therapeutic surgical code (e.g., colonoscopy with polypectomy 45385)?

**A:** Diagnostic and therapeutic colonoscopy performed during the same session:
- If the scope is performed for a specific indication (e.g., screening) and a polyp is found and removed, the therapeutic code (45385) replaces the diagnostic code (45378). Do NOT bill both.
- If the scope is performed for two separate indications in separate sessions (e.g., diagnostic by one physician and therapeutic by another), use modifier 59 on the second code.

The rule: "A diagnostic endoscopy is included in a surgical endoscopy performed on the same date."

---

**Q:** A surgeon performs a laparoscopic inguinal hernia repair on the right (49650-RT) and also repairs a left inguinal hernia during the same session (49650-LT). What is the correct coding?

**A:** 
- Option 1 (Medicare): 49650-50 (bilateral) -- one line, paid at 150%
- Option 2 (Private): Two lines, 49650-RT and 49650-LT

Either way, the bilateral hernia repair is treated as one procedure for multiple surgery rules. The total payment is 150% of the fee schedule for 49650.

---

**Q:** What are the documentation requirements for modifier 22 (Increased Procedural Service)?

**A:** Modifier 22 requires:
1. **Operative note**: Must clearly describe the increased complexity
2. **Comparison**: How this case differs from a typical procedure with the same code
3. **Extra time**: Specific additional time required
4. **Specific factors**: e.g., adhesions, obesity, anatomical variations, bleeding
5. **Cover letter**: Submitted with the claim explaining the increased work

Without adequate documentation, modifier 22 claims are routinely denied. Most require manual review by the payer.

---

**Q:** A physician performs an FNA of a breast lesion (10021) and sends it for cytopathology. The patient later undergoes a mastectomy (19303). Are these separately payable?

**A:** Yes, as long as the FNA was performed as a diagnostic step BEFORE the decision for surgery. The FNA (10021) is a diagnostic service. The mastectomy (19303) is a therapeutic procedure. They are not bundled. However, if the same surgeon performs both on the same day, the E/M for the surgery decision would use modifier 57 if a 90-day global procedure.

---

**Q:** Can an assistant surgeon bill for a procedure that has a "0-day" global period?

**A:** Yes, an assistant surgeon can bill for most surgeries regardless of the global period. However, not all procedures allow an assistant surgeon. Check the Medicare Physician Fee Schedule for "Assistant at Surgery" indicator:
- **0**: Assistant not allowed
- **1**: Assistant allowed (documentation of medical necessity may be required)
- **2**: Assistant allowed
- **9**: Assistant not covered (conceptual)

For 0-day global procedures (e.g., endoscopic procedures, arthroscopy), an assistant is typically not medically necessary and is not separately billable.

---

**Q:** What is the difference between a "staged" procedure (modifier 58) and a "related" procedure (modifier 78)?

**A:** 
- **Staged (58)**: Planned, anticipated subsequent procedure. Example: First-stage tissue expander placement, second-stage implant exchange. The second procedure was planned from the beginning.
- **Related (78)**: Unplanned return to the OR for a complication or related condition. Example: Post-op bleeding requires evacuation. The return was not planned.

The documentation for a staged procedure should reflect the planned nature (e.g., "Patient will return in 2 weeks for stage 2").

---

**Q:** How does Medicare's multiple procedure payment reduction (MPPR) affect surgical coding?

**A:** MPPR applies to:
1. **Surgical procedures**: 100%/50%/25% for same-session procedures
2. **Therapy services (PT/OT/SLP)**: 100%/50% for multiple therapy services on same day
3. **Diagnostic imaging**: 100%/50% for multiple imaging studies

For surgery, MPPR is applied automatically by Medicare's claims processing system. The provider does NOT need to append modifier 51 for Medicare, but some private payers require it.

---

**Q:** A patient has a wound dehiscence after an abdominal surgery. The surgeon returns to the OR for wound closure. What code is used?

**A:** Use the appropriate wound closure code (e.g., 12020 for treatment of wound dehiscence, simple closure) with modifier 78 (Return to OR for related procedure). If the wound is superficial (skin only), use 12020-78. If deeper layers are involved, use a more complex closure code. Document the relationship to the original surgery and that the return to OR was unplanned.

---

**Q:** When does a non-physician practitioner (PA/NP) use modifier AS vs. modifier 80?

**A:** 
- **AS**: Used by non-physician practitioners (PAs, NPs, CNSs) serving as surgical assistants. The claim is submitted under the PA's NPI.
- **80**: Used by physicians serving as assistant surgeons.

Payment for AS is at 85% of the physician assistant fee schedule, which is different from modifier 80 (20% of physician fee schedule). PAs/NPs should always use AS, not 80.

**Q:** How are bilateral procedures on the breast coded (e.g., bilateral mastectomy)?

**A:** Bilateral mastectomy (19303) is billed with modifier 50 (19303-50). Medicare pays 150% of the fee schedule (bilateral indicator 1 or 2). If the patient has a unilateral mastectomy, bill 19303-RT or 19303-LT. For skin-sparing mastectomy (19304), similar rules apply.

---

**Q:** What is the correct coding for a patient who has a mastectomy with immediate reconstruction?

**A:** The mastectomy (19303-19307) is coded by the general surgeon. The reconstruction (e.g., 19340 for immediate implant insertion or 19364 for free flap) is coded by the plastic surgeon. Both are separate procedures. Use modifier 51 for the second procedure if performed by the same surgeon, or separate claims if performed by different surgeons. The reconstruction does NOT need modifier 58 since it was planned in the same session.

**Q:** A patient has a complex exploratory laparotomy with lysis of adhesions, small bowel resection, and primary anastomosis. What codes should be used?

**A:** 
- Exploratory laparotomy with lysis of adhesions: 44005 (laparotomy, lysis of adhesions)
- Small bowel resection with anastomosis: 44120 (enterectomy with anastomosis)

If the lysis of adhesions is extensive and the primary reason for the procedure (not just incidental for access), both codes may be reportable. Use modifier 59 or -XS on 44005 if the adhesions are separate from the bowel being resected. However, many payers bundle lysis of adhesions into the resection. Check the NCCI edits.

**Q:** What is the global period for a Mohs surgery (17311-17315)?

**A:** Mohs surgery has a **000-day** global period. Each Mohs stage is separately billable, but:
- The surgical defect repair post-Mohs is separately billable (use appropriate closure code with modifier 78 or 58 depending on staging)
- Post-operative visits for the Mohs wound are included in the global period
- Same-day E/M requires modifier 25

**Q:** How do you code for a failed joint replacement that requires revision?

**A:** Use the revision codes:
- 27134 (Revision of total hip arthroplasty, both components)
- 27137 (Revision of THA, acetabular component only)
- 27138 (Revision of THA, femoral component only)
- 27487 (Revision of total knee arthroplasty, both components)
- 27486 (Revision of TKA, one component)

Revision codes pay higher than primary codes due to increased complexity. Use the appropriate diagnosis code for the reason for revision (e.g., T84.050 for mechanical loosening of hip prosthesis).

**Q:** A patient undergoes bilateral carpal tunnel release (64721) in the same session. How is this billed?

**A:** Bill 64721 with modifier 50 (64721-50) for bilateral. Carpal tunnel release has a bilateral indicator of 1 in the Medicare fee schedule. Payment is at 150% of the fee schedule. If the patient has unilateral release, use 64721-RT or 64721-LT.

**Q:** Can an E/M service and a surgical procedure be billed on the same day for a 090-day global procedure?

**A:** Yes, but the E/M must be significant and separately identifiable. Use modifier 57 if the E/M resulted in the decision to perform the surgery. If the surgery was already scheduled (e.g., pre-op visit on the day of surgery), the pre-op visit is included in the global package and is NOT separately billable. Documentation must show the E/M was distinct from the pre-operative visit.
