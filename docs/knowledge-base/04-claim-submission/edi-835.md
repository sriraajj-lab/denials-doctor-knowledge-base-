# EDI 835 (Health Care Claim Payment/Advice)

## Overview

The HIPAA 835 transaction is the electronic format used by payers to send claim payment and remittance advice information to healthcare providers. The 835 transaction replaces the paper Explanation of Benefits (EOB) or Remittance Advice (RA). It communicates how each claim was adjudicated including what was paid, denied, or adjusted.

The 835 is governed by the ASC X12N 835 (005010X221A1) standard under HIPAA. It is the primary electronic payment and remittance advice format used by Medicare, Medicaid, and most commercial payers.

---

## 1. 835 Transaction Structure

### Envelope Structure

Like the 837, the 835 is transmitted within an EDI envelope structure:

| Level | Segment | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Interchange** | ISA-IEA | Sender/receiver identification, control numbers |
| **Functional Group** | GS-GE | Application sender/receiver, version information |
| **Transaction Set** | ST-SE | Payment/remittance data for a single payer |

### 835 Transaction Layout

```
ISA ... (Interchange Control Header)
GS*HP*SENDER*RECEIVER*... (Functional Group Header, HP=Health Care Payment)
ST*835*0001 (Transaction Set Header)

BPR (Financial Information) -- total payment amount, payment method, deposit info
TRN (Reassociation Trace Number) -- Payment trace number
N1*PR (Payer Identification)
N3/N4 (Payer Address)
PER (Payer Technical Contact)
N1*PE (Payee Identification) -- Provider receiving payment
N3/N4 (Payee Address)
REF*TJ (Payee Tax Identification Number)
REF*8S (Payee Additional Identifier)

LX (Header Number) -- groups claims by payment or adjustment type

  CLP (Claim Payment Information) -- per-claim details
  NM1*QC (Patient Name)
  NM1*82 (Rendering Provider)
  NM1*77 (Attending Provider)
  REF (Claim ID, Medical Record Number)
  DTP (Date of Service)
  AMT*AU (Claim-Level Claim Adjustment)
  CAS (Claim Adjustment Segment) -- adjustments at claim level

  SVC (Service Payment Information) -- per-service line details
  DTP (Line-Level Date)
  CAS (Service Adjustment Segment) -- adjustments at line level
  REF (Line Item Reference)
  LQ (Line Remark)

  ...

  SE*...*0001 (Transaction Set Trailer)
GE*1*... (Functional Group Trailer)
IEA*... (Interchange Control Trailer)
```

## 4. CAS Adjustment Claim Adjustment Group Logic

### CO (Contractual Obligation) Adjustments

CO adjustments represent amounts the provider contractually agrees not to bill. These are typically contractual write-offs.

| Use Case | CARC | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Fee schedule discount | 45 | Charge exceeds fee schedule or maximum allowable |
| Non-covered service | 96 | Service is not covered under the benefit plan |
| Bundled service | 97 | Service is bundled into another service's payment |
| Prior auth missing | 51 | No prior authorization obtained, payment reduced |
| Medical necessity | 50 | Service not medically necessary |

### PR (Patient Responsibility) Adjustments

PR adjustments represent amounts that are the patient's financial responsibility.

| Use Case | CARC | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Deductible | 1 | Patient deductible not yet met |
| Co-insurance | 2 | Patient's co-insurance portion |
| Co-payment | 3 | Patient's co-payment amount |
| Non-covered service | 96 | Service not covered, patient responsible (with ABN) |

### OA (Other Adjustment) Adjustments

OA adjustments represent amounts that do not fit into contractual or patient responsibility.

| Use Case | CARC | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Provider discount | 18 | Provider-specific discount |
| Credit balance | 154 | Overpayment recovery |
| Penalty | 201 | Filing penalty or late submission penalty |

### PI (Payer Initiated Reductions)

PI adjustments are reductions initiated by the payer, not the provider's patient.

| Use Case | CARC | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| MUE reduction | 176 | Exceeded Medically Unlikely Edit |
| Payer downcoding | 151 | Service level not supported |
| Multiple procedure rule | 59 | Multiple surgery reduction |

### CR (Corrections and Reversals)

CR adjustments correct or reverse previously applied adjustments.

| Use Case | CARC | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Adjustment reversal | A1 | Reversal of a previous adjustment |
| Adjustment correction | A2 | Correction to a previous adjustment |
| Data correction | A3 | Correction of data elements |

## 5. COB Processing in the 835

### Primary Claim

When a claim is processed as primary (CLP02 = 1), the 835 shows:
- CLP04 = Total payment from primary payer
- CAS segments show contractual and patient responsibility amounts
- No COB-specific adjustment codes needed

### Secondary Claim

When a claim is processed as secondary (CLP02 = 2), the 835 includes:
- CLP04 = Secondary payment (may be zero if primary already paid at or above allowable)
- CAS segments with CARC 23 or 150 showing the primary payment as an Other Adjustment
- CAS segments showing patient responsibility differences after primary payment

### Tertiary Claim

When a claim is processed as tertiary (CLP02 = 3), the 835 shows:
- CLP04 = Tertiary payment
- CAS segments with adjustments for both primary and secondary payments

### COB Example

```xml
CLP*12345*2*150*50*20***2025000001
  -- Claim 12345 processed as secondary
  -- Total charges: $150
  -- Secondary payment: $50
  -- Patient responsibility: $20

CAS*OA*23*75
  -- Other Adjustment: $75 (primary payment applied)

CAS*PR*1*15
  -- Patient Responsibility: $15 deductible not applied yet

CAS*PR*2*5
  -- Patient Responsibility: $5 coinsurance
```

## 6. Paper Remittance vs Electronic 835

### Paper Remittance (ERAs)

Traditional paper Explanation of Benefits (EOB) or Remittance Advice (RA) documents:

| Aspect | Paper RA | Electronic 835 |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| **Format** | Printed document | EDI X12 standard |
| **Delivery** | Mail, fax, or portal download | EDI transmission, direct download |
| **Processing** | Manual data entry required | Automatic posting possible |
| **Accuracy** | Subject to human error | Machine-readable, fewer errors |
| **Turnaround** | Slower (mail delays) | Faster (electronic delivery) |
| **Storage** | Physical filing required | Digital storage |
| **Searchability** | Manual search | Electronic search |
| **Cost** | Paper, printing, postage | No physical materials |
| **Compliance** | Less efficient for audits | Easier to audit and track |

### Medicare RA vs EFT/ERA

Medicare provides both paper and electronic remittance:

| Option | RA Delivery | Payment | Fee Schedule |
|--------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| **Paper RA + Paper Check** | Mail | Paper check | Standard |
| **Paper RA + EFT** | Mail | Electronic deposit | Standard |
| **ERA (835) + EFT** | Electronic | Electronic deposit | Reduced or eliminated |
| **ERA (835) + Paper Check** | Electronic | Paper check | Standard |

Medicare encourages providers to enroll in EFT/ERA (Electronic Funds Transfer / Electronic Remittance Advice). Providers who use both EFT and ERA typically receive payments faster and have lower administrative costs.

## 7. Reading and Interpreting the 835

### Step-by-Step Interpretation

1. **BPR Segment**: Determine total payment amount and payment method. Is this a payment, a reversal, or a notification only?
2. **TRN Segment**: Get the trace number to reconcile with the EFT deposit.
3. **N1 Segments**: Identify the payer and the payee.
4. **LX Segment**: Identify the payment grouping (payment, denial, correction).
5. **CLP Segments**: For each claim, identify:
   - Claim status (paid, denied, partial, reversed)
   - Total charges, payment amount, patient responsibility
   - Claim control number
6. **CAS Segments**: For each claim, identify:
   - What adjustments were applied
   - Group codes (CO, PR, OA, PI, CR)
   - Adjustment reason codes and amounts
7. **SVC Segments**: For each claim, identify:
   - Which procedure codes were paid, denied, or adjusted
   - Line-level adjustments
   - Units paid vs units billed

### Key Questions When Reading a 835

1. Was the claim paid, denied, or partially paid?
2. If denied: what is the denial reason code (CARC)?
3. If adjusted: what adjustment group and reason code applies?
4. Is the patient responsible for any portion (PR group codes)?
5. Are there contractual adjustments (CO group codes) that need to be posted?
6. Does the total of payments + adjustments equal the total charges?
7. Are there any reversed claims (CLP02 = 7 or 22)?
8. Are there any corrections or reversals (CR group codes)?

## 8. 835 Reconciliation

### Payment Reconciliation Process

```
Total Charges (from 837)
  |
  +-- Paid Amount (CLP04) -- Posted as payment
  |
  +-- Contractual Adjustments (CO group) -- Written off
  |     |-- Fee schedule reductions
  |     |-- Bundled service reductions
  |     |-- Multiple procedure reductions
  |
  +-- Patient Responsibility (PR group) -- Billed to patient
  |     |-- Deductible
  |     |-- Co-insurance
  |     |-- Co-pay
  |     |-- Non-covered (with valid ABN)
  |
  +-- Other Adjustments (OA group) -- Posted as adjustments
  |     |-- Primary payer payment (secondary claims)
  |     |-- Provider discounts
  |
  +-- Payer Initiated Reductions (PI group) -- Posted as adjustments
        |-- MUE reductions
        |-- Downcoding reductions
```

### Balancing Equation

```
Total Charges (CLP03) = 
  Payment Amount (CLP04) + 
  Total CAS Adjustments + 
  Patient Responsibility (CLP05)
```

## 9. Agent Training: Q&A Pairs for EDI 835

**Q: What is the purpose of the 835 transaction?**

**A:** The 835 (Health Care Claim Payment/Advice) transaction is the electronic format used by payers to send claim payment information and remittance advice to healthcare providers. It communicates claim adjudication results including payments made, services denied, adjustments applied, and patient responsibility amounts. The 835 replaces the paper Explanation of Benefits (EOB) or Remittance Advice (RA). It enables automated payment posting and reduces manual data entry compared to paper remittances.

---

**Q: What does the CLP02 status code of 4 indicate?**

**A:** CLP02 status code 4 indicates the claim was "Denied." The claim was processed and denied in full -- no payment will be made. The provider should refer to the CAS segments within the same CLP loop to understand the specific denial reason code (CARC), such as CARC 50 (not medically necessary), CARC 51 (no prior authorization), or CARC 96 (non-covered service). A denied claim may be appealed or resubmitted with corrected information within the timely filing limits.

---

**Q: What is the difference between a CO group adjustment and a PR group adjustment?**

**A:** A CO (Contractual Obligation) group adjustment represents amounts the provider is contractually required to write off per the agreement with the payer. These are non-billable to the patient. A PR (Patient Responsibility) group adjustment represents amounts the patient is financially responsible for, such as deductible, co-insurance, and co-pay amounts. The provider can bill the patient for PR adjustments but not for CO adjustments. The distinction is critical for compliance -- billing a patient for CO adjustments is a violation of the provider's contract with the payer.

---

**Q: What is the purpose of the CAS segment in the 835?**

**A:** The CAS (Claim Adjustment Segment) reports adjustments applied to the claim at the claim level (when appearing after the CLP) or at the service line level (when appearing after the SVC segment). Each CAS instance reports a claim adjustment group code (CO, PR, OA, PI, CR), an adjustment reason code (CARC), an adjustment amount, and optionally an adjustment quantity. The CAS segment is how the 835 communicates why a claim was not paid in full, including denials, reductions, and patient liability.

---

**Q: How does a provider identify which claim an 835 remittance is for?**

**A:** The provider identifies the claim by reading the CLP01 element (Claim Submitter's Identifier), which contains the patient control number that the provider assigned in the original 837 submission (from CLM01). The CLP07 element (Payer Claim Control Number) contains the payer's unique identifier for the claim. The provider can also cross-reference the patient name in the NM1*QC segment, the date of service in the DTP segment, and the procedure codes in the SVC segment to positively match the 835 to the original claim.

---

**Q: What does CARC 45 mean in an 835 adjustment?**

**A:** CARC (Claim Adjustment Reason Code) 45 means "Charge exceeds fee schedule/maximum allowable." This is a CO (Contractual Obligation) adjustment. The provider's submitted charge was higher than the payer's allowed amount for that procedure code. For example, if a provider billed $150 for a service with a fee schedule of $100, the 835 would show a CAS segment with group CO, reason code 45, and an adjustment amount of $50. The provider writes off the $50 difference as a contractual adjustment.

---

**Q: What is the function of the BPR segment?**

**A:** The BPR (Financial Information) segment is the first data segment in the 835 and provides the overall payment information. It includes the total payment amount, the payment method (EFT, check, or non-payment), the sender's financial institution information, the receiver's account number, the EFT trace number, and the effective date of the payment. The BPR segment gives the provider the summary-level financial information needed to reconcile the payment to the bank deposit.

---

**Q: How does a 835 handle reversals of previously paid claims?**

**A:** A reversal of a previously paid claim is indicated by CLP02 status code 7 (Reversed) or 22 (Reversal of Previous Payment). The CLP01 identifier matches the original claim identifier, and the CLP04 payment amount shows a negative value (the amount being recovered). CAS segments with group code CR (Corrections and Reversals) explain the reason for the reversal. The provider must post the reversal in their system, typically as a credit adjustment or a refund request to the payer.

---

**Q: What information is found in the SVC segment of the 835?**

**A:** The SVC (Service Payment Information) segment contains payment information for each individual service line on the claim. It includes the procedure code (CPT/HCPCS) with qualifier, up to four modifiers, the line item charge amount billed, the line item payment amount, the revenue code (for institutional claims), the number of units billed, and the number of units paid. Line-level CAS segments following the SVC provide adjustment detail specific to that service line.

---

**Q: What is the TRN segment and why is it important?**

**A:** The TRN (Reassociation Trace Number) segment contains the trace number that links the electronic payment (EFT) to the 835 remittance document. This trace number must match between the EFT notification and the 835 for the provider to reconcile the payment to the remittance. The TRN segment includes the trace number assigned by the payer or the clearinghouse and the originating company identifier. Without the TRN segment, the provider cannot automatically match the EFT deposit to the 835 remittance.

---

**Q: How does the 835 indicate that a claim was partially paid?**

**A:** A partially paid claim is indicated by CLP02 status code 20 (Claim is partially paid). The CLP03 shows the total charge amount, CLP04 shows the partial payment, and CAS segments explain why the full amount was not paid. For example, a claim with $150 in charges might show CLP04 = $100 (partial payment) with a CAS segment showing a CO adjustment of $50 for a contractual discount. The provider posts the $100 payment and the $50 contractual adjustment.

---

**Q: What is the difference between a CLP-level adjustment and an SVC-level adjustment?**

**A:** A CLP-level adjustment is reported in a CAS segment that appears directly after the CLP segment. This adjustment applies to the entire claim and is not attributed to any specific service line. An SVC-level adjustment is reported in a CAS segment that appears after an SVC segment. This adjustment applies to that specific service line only. Typically, contractual adjustments (CO group) are reported at the SVC level for line-specific edits, while patient responsibility (PR group) adjustments may be reported at the CLP level for claim-wide amounts like deductible.

---

**Q: What is CARC 150 and when is it used?**

**A:** CARC 150 means "Payment made by primary payer." It is an OA (Other Adjustment) group code used on secondary or tertiary claims to report the amount paid by the previous payer. For example, on a secondary claim, the 835 might show CAS*OA*150*75 to indicate the primary payer paid $75. This adjustment does not affect the provider's net payment for this payer -- it simply reflects the primary payer's payment. The provider posts this as informational and posts the secondary payment separately.

---

**Q: What should a provider do when they receive a 835 with a CLP02 status of 4 (Denied)?**

**A:** When a provider receives a 835 with CLP02 = 4 (Denied), they should: read the CAS segments to identify the specific denial reason (CARC code), review the corresponding claim to understand why it was denied, determine whether the denial can be appealed or if the claim needs to be corrected and resubmitted, check if an ABN was signed (for Medicare claims) to determine patient liability, take appropriate action (file an appeal, correct and resubmit, or write off the service), and track the denial reason for trend analysis and process improvement.

---

**Q: How does the 835 handle EFT-only notification (no payment)?**

**A:** When the 835 is sent with no accompanying payment (notification only), the BPR01 payment method code is typically set to "NON" (Non-Payment Data) or "H" (Notification Only). The BPR02 total payment amount is $0.00. This scenario occurs when the 835 is reporting adjustments, denials, or reversals without an associated electronic payment. The provider should still post the adjustments and denial information even though no payment was received.

---

**Q: What is the purpose of the N1*PE segment in the 835?**

**A:** The N1*PE segment identifies the payee (the entity receiving the payment) in Loop 1000B. It includes the payee's name, NPI, and other identifiers. This is typically the billing provider or the provider's payment address. The REF*TJ segment following N1*PE contains the payee's tax identification number (TIN), and REF*8S contains the payee's provider identifier as assigned by the payer. The provider should verify that the N1*PE information matches their records to ensure the payment was correctly directed.

---

**Q: What is CARC 252 and what does it mean?**

**A:** CARC 252 means "Payment denied/reduced because a modifier is needed." This indicates that the claim line was denied because a modifier was required to separate the procedure from another service on the same claim, but no modifier was present. The appropriate course of action depends on the specific modifier needed. If modifier 59, XE, XS, XP, or XU would have been appropriate, the provider can correct the claim by adding the appropriate modifier and resubmitting. This CARC is commonly seen when a claim passes NCCI edits but the payer's system requires a modifier for line separation.

---

**Q: How is CARC 59 (Multiple Procedure Rule) reported in the 835?**

**A:** CARC 59 means "Processed based on multiple procedure rule." This adjustment applies when multiple surgical procedures are performed during the same session. The primary procedure is paid at 100%, and subsequent procedures are reduced (typically to 50%). The 835 shows the reduction as a CO group adjustment with CARC 59 on the reduced procedure lines. For example, if procedures A ($100) and B ($75) are performed, and procedure B is reduced to $37.50, the 835 would show CAS*CO*59*37.50 on procedure B's SVC loop.

---

**Q: What does it mean when the 835 shows a negative amount in CLP04?**

**A:** A negative amount in CLP04 indicates a reversal of a previously paid claim. This happens when the payer recovers a previous overpayment. The CLP02 status code will typically be 7 (Reversed) or 22 (Reversal of Previous Payment). The provider must post the negative payment as a credit against their accounts receivable, and may need to issue a refund to the payer or have the negative amount offset against future payments. The CAS segment with the CR group code explains the reason for the reversal.

---

**Q: How does a provider reconcile the 835 to the EFT deposit?**

**A:** The provider reconciles the 835 to the EFT deposit by: reading the BPR segment to find the total payment amount, the EFT trace number (BPR09), and the deposit account number (BPR07); finding the corresponding EFT deposit in their bank account by matching the trace number or amount; using the TRN segment to confirm the trace number match; reading the N1*PE segment to confirm the payee matches; and posting each claim's payment from the CLP segments to the corresponding patient accounts.

---

**Q: What is the LX segment in the 835?**

**A:** The LX (Header Number) segment groups related CLP segments together within the 835. Each LX segment starts a new group of claims. Typically, all paid claims are grouped under one LX, denied claims under another, and reversals under a third. The LX segment helps organize the 835 into logical groups. Some providers use the LX grouping for automated posting logic, although the LX grouping is not always used or may be organized differently by different payers.

---

**Q: What is CARC 11 (Diagnosis inconsistent with procedure)?**

**A:** CARC 11 is a CO (Contractual Obligation) adjustment that means the diagnosis code submitted on the claim is not clinically consistent with the procedure code billed. For example, billing a knee MRI with a diagnosis of low back pain would result in CARC 11. This is a medical necessity denial. The provider can appeal by submitting medical records that establish the diagnosis-to-procedure relationship, or correct the claim with a more appropriate diagnosis code if the original was incorrect.

---

**Q: How does a provider handle a 835 with CLP02 status 2 (Processed as Secondary)?**

**A:** When a provider receives a 835 with CLP02 = 2 (Processed as Secondary), the claim was submitted as a secondary claim (after primary processing). The provider should: verify that the primary claim was processed by the primary payer; check the OA group CAS segment with CARC 150 (Payment made by primary payer) for the primary payment amount; post the primary payment as a payment from the primary payer; post the secondary payment from the CLP04 of this 835; and bill any PR (patient responsibility) amounts to the patient. The sum of primary payment + secondary payment + adjustments should equal the total charges.

---

**Q: What is the difference between a 997 and a 835?**

**A:** A 997 (Functional Acknowledgment) is a technical acknowledgment that confirms the 837 submission was received and was syntactically valid. It does not contain any claim adjudication information -- it only confirms the EDI transmission was valid. A 835 (Payment/Advice) is the adjudication result that shows payment and adjustment information for each claim. The 997 is returned within minutes to hours, while the 835 is returned after adjudication, typically 7-30 days after claim submission. The 997 tells you the file was received; the 835 tells you what was paid.

---

**Q: What is CARC 97 and when is it used?**

**A:** CARC 97 means "The benefit for this service is included in the payment/allowance for another service." This is a CO (Contractual Obligation) adjustment. It indicates that the procedure was bundled into another service on the claim. For example, if CPT 93005 (ECG tracing) is billed with CPT 93000 (complete ECG), the 835 would adjust 93005 with CARC 97, since the tracing is included in the complete ECG code. The provider should not bill the patient for CARC 97 adjustments.

---

**Q: How does the 835 process a claim that was corrected by the payer?**

**A:** When a payer corrects a submitted code (downcoding), the 835 shows the corrected code in the SVC segment and the original submitted code in the REF segment with qualifier 6R. The CAS segment shows the adjustment reason, such as CARC 151 (payer deems the information submitted does not support this level of service). For example, if the provider submitted 99214 (established patient level 4) and the payer downcoded to 99213 (level 3), the SVC segment would show 99213, and the adjustment would be under group CO with CARC 151.

---

**Q: What is the role of the DTP segment in the 835?**

**A:** The DTP (Date/Time Period) segment in the 835 reports dates associated with the claim or service line. Common DTP qualifiers include: 232 (Service Date), 233 (Service Date Range), 234 (Admission Date), 235 (Discharge Date), 036 (Date of Service), 050 (Payment Effective Date), and 573 (Date of Primary Payment). The DTP segments help the provider match the 835 to the correct claims by date of service.

---

**Q: What should a provider do if the 835 total payment amount (BPR02) does not match the bank deposit?**

**A:** If the 835 total payment amount (BPR02) does not match the bank deposit, the provider should: verify the deposit date -- payments may take 1-2 business days to appear; check for multiple 835s that relate to a single deposit -- some payers combine multiple 835s into one EFT; verify the TRN trace number on the 835 matches the EFT trace number; contact the payer's provider service line for help with reconciliation; contact the bank to confirm the deposit details; and examine the 835 for reversals or adjustments that could explain the difference.

---

**Q: How is a deductible reported in the 835?**

**A:** A deductible is reported in the PR (Patient Responsibility) group CAS segment with CARC 1 (Deductible Amount). For example, CAS*PR*1*100 indicates the patient has a $100 deductible that has been applied. The patient is responsible for paying this $100 to the provider. The deductible may appear at the claim level (after CLP) or the service line level (after SVC). The deductible amount is also included in CLP05 (Patient Responsibility Amount), along with co-insurance and co-pay amounts.

---

**Q: What is the difference between a "processed as primary" claim and a "denied" claim in the 835?**

**A:** A "processed as primary" claim (CLP02 = 1) means the claim was adjudicated and the payer accepted it as the primary payer. The claim was either paid in full, partially paid, or adjusted. A "denied" claim (CLP02 = 4) means the claim was not paid at all -- the payer determined no payment is due under the patient's benefit plan. A denied claim may be appealed or corrected and resubmitted. A processed-as-primary claim with adjustments has been paid (at least partially) and the provider posts whatever payment was made.

---

**Q: What is CARC 6 (Pre-existing condition)?**

**A:** CARC 6 means "Pre-existing condition." This is a CO (Contractual Obligation) adjustment indicating that the service was denied because the patient's condition was pre-existing and not covered under the benefit plan. This adjustment applies when the patient's policy has a pre-existing condition exclusion period. CARC 6 is less common under the Affordable Care Act, which prohibits pre-existing condition exclusions for most plans, but may still apply to grandfathered plans or non-ACA-compliant plans.
